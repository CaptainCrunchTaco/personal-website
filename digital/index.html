Kids With Dreams

Summer of 2014 I was contacted by Kids with Dreams (a student group that lead activities involving disabled children) to redesign their website. It was aimed at informing students and parents of the organization and about upcoming events. The site they previously had was from early 2000s and they needed something new to conform to the new standards. I decided to use bootstrap to make thier site mobile-friendly since it will be mostly accessed by students and parents in Silicon Valley. It is currently a work in progress and undergoing changes so stay tuned. KWD Website

SwitchMate

SwitchMate was a mechatronics project that involved working with Android devices. SwitchMate is a device you magnetically attach to light switches. This device can be actuated from your phone to physically flick switches in your home.

On this team, I focused on wireframes and logo designs for our application. Here you can see our logo (a circuit diagram symbol for a switch) and the menus used to find devices via bluetooth and add devices to your home screen.

We went on to demo this product to a few VCs and entrepreneurs in a friendly competition in our class. We were judged as the most impressive team and won a free dinner (nom nom nom).

FlameStower

I worked at a small start-up company called FlameStower.

They charge phones using fire.

At FlameStower, I did a lot of things. I built a full fledged website and integrated third-party shopping cart software so that the site could start accepting pre-orders. I took photographs of the product in use to populate the website and our social media pages. And I did a lot of materials strength tests to see which alloys would work best under intense heat. But mainly I focused on the web page. Ah, the good ole start up days. After I left, the website went through many revisions.
